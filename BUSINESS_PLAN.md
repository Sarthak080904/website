# Dropshipping / Print-On-Demand Store Plan

## Recommended Model

Use Shopify as the storefront and checkout. Use a print-on-demand supplier for production and shipping.

Customer flow:

1. Customer visits the Shopify website.
2. Customer selects a product, color, size, and design option.
3. Customer pays through Shopify checkout.
4. The order is sent to the POD supplier by app, API, CSV export, or manual placement.
5. Supplier prints, packs, ships, and provides tracking.
6. Shopify sends the customer order and shipping updates.

## Best First Product Categories

Start broad enough to test demand, but keep operations simple:

- T-shirts and oversized tees
- Hoodies and sweatshirts
- Tote bags
- Mugs and drinkware
- Phone cases
- Posters and stickers
- Caps
- Business cards, flyers, and banners if supplier turnaround is reliable

Avoid launching too many suppliers at once. One strong supplier with reliable print quality, tracking, and replacement policy is better than a huge catalog that is hard to manage.

## Supplier Integration Options

Best case:

- Supplier has a Shopify app.
- Product variants, mockups, orders, fulfillment, and tracking sync automatically.

Middle case:

- Supplier accepts CSV/API orders.
- Shopify collects the order, then you export or automate order submission.

Manual starting case:

- You place each paid order manually on the supplier website.
- This is slower but good for validation before investing in automation.

## Website Priorities

- Homepage must immediately explain custom products and made-to-order fulfillment.
- Product pages must show sizing, material, print position, delivery estimate, and return limitations.
- Custom products need upload or instruction fields.
- Cart must preserve uploaded artwork and custom notes.
- Trust sections should explain printing, quality checks, shipping, and support.

## Operations Checklist

- Choose one primary POD supplier.
- Order samples before selling publicly.
- Decide product margin after supplier cost, Shopify fees, payment fees, ads, packaging, and replacement losses.
- Write policies for shipping time, design proofing, damaged item replacements, and custom-order returns.
- Create collections by category.
- Import products with clean names, descriptions, variants, and mockups.
- Test one full checkout and fulfillment order before launch.

## Website Pages To Create In Shopify Admin

The theme includes templates for these pages, but Shopify page records still need
to be created in Admin:

- Custom Orders: use template `page.custom-orders`
- Artwork Guide: use template `page.artwork-guide`
- Shipping & Returns: use template `page.shipping-returns`
- About: use template `page.about`
- FAQ: use template `page.faq`
- Size Guide: use template `page.size-guide`
- Contact: use template `page.contact`

After creating pages, add them to the main menu and footer menu.

## Brand Direction

The current theme direction should feel modern, fast, clean, and creative rather than cheap or generic. Use strong product imagery, short copy, clear CTAs, and visible proof that products are printed only after the customer orders.
